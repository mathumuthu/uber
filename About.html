THE BEGINNING

From theKarrier to Rapido

Rapido was founded in November 2015 by three IIT alumni — Aravind Sanka (CEO), Pavan Guntupalli, and SR Rishikesh. Originally started as "theKarrier," a B2B logistics platform for intra-city truck aggregation, the founders quickly realized that shifting to B2C offered more growth potential.

The pivot to bike taxis came from a simple observation: Indian cities were choked with traffic, and millions of people needed an affordable, quick way to commute. While Ola and Uber dominated the cab market, they were too expensive for daily use by the masses.

With just 15-20 bikes in Bangalore, Rapido was born with a mission to make urban commuting affordable, accessible, and efficient for every Indian.

The Problem

Traffic congestion in Indian cities

The Insight

20 crore bikes in India, but no organized bike taxi service

The Solution

Bike taxis - fast, affordable, efficient

The Result

India's #1 bike taxi platform

WHY WE EXIST

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To make daily commuting more affordable, convenient, and accessible for every Indian. We aim to solve urban mobility challenges through technology, emphasizing speed, cost-effectiveness, and user safety.

We believe that reliable transportation is a right, not a privilege. By leveraging India's existing two-wheeler ecosystem, we create income opportunities for millions while providing affordable rides to billions.

Our Vision

To become the leading mobility platform for Bharat, serving every city and town in India. We envision a future where every commute is seamless, safe, and sustainable.

We're building for Bharat — focusing on Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities where 40% of our users experience ride-sharing for the first time through Rapido. Our goal is to democratize urban mobility across the nation.

OUR JOURNEY

Milestones & Achievements

2015

The Beginning

Founded as theKarrier in Bangalore by Aravind Sanka, Pavan Guntupalli, and Rishikesh SR. Pivoted to Rapido with 15-20 bikes.

2016

First Investment

After 75 rejections, secured funding from Pawan Munjal (Hero MotoCorp). Expanded to Gurugram, focusing on Tier 2 & 3 cities.

2018

Rapid Expansion

Crossed 15,000 registered captains and 30,000 daily rides. Rajan Anandan (former Google India head) invested in the company.

2019

1 Crore Users

Reached 1 crore registered users. Created over 500,000 jobs for captains across India. Expanded to 25+ cities.

2020

Diversification

Launched Rapido Auto (on-demand auto rickshaws). Pivoted to logistics during COVID-19, delivering essentials for BigBasket, Swiggy, etc.

2022

Major Funding

Raised $180 million in Series D led by Swiggy and TVS Motor. Valuation reached $830 million. Expanded to 100+ cities.

2023

Cab Service Launch

Introduced taxicab hailing on the app, directly competing with Ola and Uber. Launched Bike Pink for women commuters.

2024

🦄 Unicorn Status

Raised $200 million in Series E at $1.1 billion valuation. Joined the unicorn club. Serving 25M+ users with 2.5M daily orders.
